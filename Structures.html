🚀 The Mission

Welcome, Engineers. Your goal today is to decode the invisible forces that keep the world standing. Using the provided guide and your research, you will complete this structural portfolio in pairs.

  • Time: 80 Minutes
  • Tool: Canva Presentation / Webpage
  • Colors: Navy Blue, Forest Green, White, Gray

🧠 Stage 1: The Visual Mind Map (20 min)

Create a central Mind Map on your first slide. You must categorize structures based on how they support loads[cite: 5]:

  • Mass Structures: Focus on weight and gravity (e.g., Dams, Pyramids)[cite: 5, 8].
  • Frame Structures: Focus on skeletons and load paths (e.g., Human skeleton, Skyscrapers)[cite: 5, 10].
  • Shell Structures: Focus on curvature and thin skins (e.g., Eggs, Helmets)[cite: 6, 13].
  • Hybrids: Complex modern objects (e.g., Cars)[cite: 7, 46].

✍️ Stage 2: The 10-Question Challenge (40 min)

Answer these questions using technical diagrams and clear English descriptions:

  • 1. Definition: What is the primary job of a "Structure" regarding loads? [cite: 5, 39]
  • 2. Stability: Why do Mass Structures require a "broad base" to resist water pressure or gravity? [cite: 9, 53]
  • 3. Shell Power: How does "curvature" allow a thin material to become rigid? [cite: 13, 14]
  • 4. Force Duo: Define Tension and Compression. Use arrows to show their direction[cite: 17, 88].
  • 5. Anatomy: In a frame, what is the specific job of a Tie and a Strut? [cite: 16, 77]
  • 6. Geometry: Why is the triangle considered the strongest shape in engineering? [cite: 19, 24]
  • 7. Joints: What is a Gusset Plate and how does it prevent a frame from collapsing? [cite: 20, 23]
  • 8. Efficiency: What is the "Neutral Axis" of an I-Beam and why is there no material there? [cite: 27, 28]
  • 9. Evolution: Explain the difference between a 2D Truss and a 3D Space Frame[cite: 24, 25].
  • 10. Innovation: Why is a "Sandwich" composite better than a solid heavy beam? [cite: 30, 31]

🔍 Stage 3: Case Study (10 min)

Find a photo of a famous structure (Eiffel Tower, Burj Khalifa, or a Bike Frame). Label at least 3 parts using the terms: Triangulation, Tension, Compression, or Shell.
