The Super Simple D&T Project Guide

PART 1: Learning the Words (Using ChatGPT)

Step 1: Open ChatGPT
  1. Go to your computer.
  2. Open your internet browser (the icon for Google Chrome, Safari, Firefox, or Edge).
  3. Click on the long white bar at the very top of the screen (the address bar).
  4. Type this exactly: chat.openai.com
  5. Press the Enter key on your keyboard.
Step 2: Log in to ChatGPT
  1. You will see a webpage. Find the button that says "Log in". Click it.
  2. Type your email address in the box.
  3. Type your password in the next box.
  4. Click the "Continue" button.
  5. (If you do not have an account, you must click "Sign up" and create one first. Then come back to this step).
Step 3: Create new conversation
  1. After you log in, look at the top-left corner of the screen.
  2. You will see a button that says "New Chat" or looks like a pencil icon.
  3. Click that button. This gives you a blank, fresh page so the AI (the computer) does not get confused by old conversations.
Step 4: Activate Study and Learn mode
  1. Look near the top of the screen where you might see "GPT-4" or a "+" icon.
  2. Click on the "+" button or the menu.
  3. A list will appear. Find the option that says "Study and Learn". Click it.
  4. This tells the computer to act like a teacher for your specific IGCSE subject.
Step 5: Introduce the prompt
  1. A "prompt" is just the instructions you type.
  2. Click inside the big text box at the bottom of the screen that says "Message...".
  3. Type exactly this:
    I need you to explain to me from the point of view of IGCSE 0445 Design & Technology what ______________ is and how it is used. (place the topic to study in the blank space)
  4. VERY IMPORTANT: Now, use your mouse to highlight and delete the ______________ (the blank line).
  5. In that exact spot, type the first topic you need to learn. For example, type ACCESSFM.
  6. Your full prompt will look like this: I need you to explain to me from the point of view of IGCSE 0445 Design & Technology what ACCESSFM is and how it is used.
  7. Press the Enter key on your keyboard.
Step 6: Read, understand, ask, and interact
  1. ChatGPT will write a lot of words. Your job is to read all of them.
  2. If you read a word you don't know, ask about it. Type: What does the word "aesthetics" mean?
  3. If you are confused, tell it. Type: Can you explain the "Function" part again, but simpler?
  4. Keep asking questions until your brain says, "Oh, okay, I finally get it now."
Step 7: Provide a definition and uses...
  1. Now it's your turn to prove you learned it.
  2. In the same chat box, type exactly this prompt:
    Ok, let me see if I understood: (here goes your DEFINITION AND USE of the topic), verify if it is right or not, if it is right, tell me that I can now put it in the Word doc, if it is wrong, only tell me what I should improve, do not give me a new definition but explain where I am going wrong
  3. VERY IMPORTANT: Highlight and delete the text that says (here goes your DEFINITION AND USE of the topic).
  4. In that exact spot, type what you learned in your own words.
  5. Example: Ok, let me see if I understood: ACCESSFM is a checklist to make sure your design is good. It checks if it looks nice, how much it costs, if it's safe, and if it actually works. Is that right?
  6. Press Enter and read the computer's answer. If it says you are right, you are ready for the next topic. If it says you are wrong, read why you are wrong and try Step 7 again.
Step 8: Repeat in a new conversation for each topic
  1. This is a very important step. You are now going to learn the next topic.
  2. Go back and do Step 3 (Create new conversation). This is required.
  3. Do Step 4 (Activate Study and Learn mode) again.
  4. Do Step 5 (Introduce the prompt), but this time, in the blank space, type the next topic from the list (for example, SWOT analysis).
  5. Do Step 6 (Read and understand).
  6. Do Step 7 (Provide your definition).
Steps 9-14: The Topics To-Do List

These are not actions. This is your "to-do" list. You must do Steps 3 through 8 for every single one of these 6 topics:

  • ACCESSFM
  • SWOT analysis
  • Design Brief
  • Design Specs
  • Data Analysis
  • User Research

When you have 6 separate conversations and you understand all 6 topics, you are ready for Part 2.

PART 2: Creating Your Project Document

Step 15: Create a Word DOC
  1. Go to your computer's applications or programs.
  2. Find and open Microsoft Word (the program with the blue "W" icon).
  3. Choose "Blank document".
  4. At the top, click "File", then "Save As".
  5. Name your file something you will remember, like D&T Project - My Name.docx.
  6. Save it to your Desktop or a school folder where you can find it.
Step 16: By using styles, organize the Word doc...

This step is about making your document look professional and organized.

  1. To create an Index (Table of Contents):
    • Click the "References" tab at the top of Word.
    • Click the "Table of Contents" button on the far left.
    • Choose a style. It will probably say "No entries found." That is OK. We will add them next.
  2. To use Titles (Title, Title 1, Title 2):
    • Click on the "Home" tab at the top.
    • Find the box called "Styles" (it has examples like Normal, Heading 1, Heading 2).
    • When you type a main title for a new section (like Research), highlight the word and click on "Heading 1" in the Styles box.
    • When you type a sub-title (like My Chosen Methodology), highlight it and click "Heading 2".
  3. To add Header and Footer:
    • Click the "Insert" tab at the top. Find and click the "Header" button. Choose a style and type your name.
    • Click the "Insert" tab again. Find and click the "Footer" button, then the "Page Number" option.
Step 17: Create a new section titled "Application"
  1. In your Word document, press the Enter key until you are on a new, blank page.
  2. Type the word: Application
  3. Highlight this word with your mouse.
  4. Go to the "Home" tab and click "Heading 1" from the Styles box.
Step 18: Select one methodology
  1. In Part 1, you learned about ACCESSFM and SWOT analysis.
  2. You must now choose one of them to use for your project. You cannot use both.
  3. Pick the one you understood the best or that makes the most sense for your project.
Step 19: Explain why you chose that
  1. In your Word document, click under your "Application" title.
  2. Type a new sub-title: My Chosen Methodology
  3. Highlight this text and click "Heading 2" from the Styles box.
  4. Under this new sub-title, write 2-3 sentences explaining why you picked the one you picked.
  5. Example: I chose to use ACCESSFM. I picked this methodology because my project is a physical sign, and ACCESSFM covers all the details I need to think about, like materials, size, and safety.
Step 20: Create a survey (max 3 questions)
  1. You need to ask people what they think of your original sign (the "bad" one you are going to fix).
  2. Think of 3 very simple questions. The prompt gives you examples.
  3. Good example questions:
    1. What is the first thing you think this sign is trying to tell you?
    2. What part of this sign is the most confusing?
    3. If you could change one thing to make it better, what would you change?
  4. Type these 3 questions into your Word document (in a new section called "User Research") so you don't forget them.
Step 21: Ask MAXIMUM 4 people those questions
  1. Find 4 people. You can ask 1, 2, or 3 people, but do not ask more than 4.
  2. Show each person your original sign (the "bad" one).
  3. Ask them your 3 questions from Step 20.
  4. In your Word document, create a new section. Type USER RESEARCH and make it a "Heading 1".
  5. Under that title, write down exactly what each person said.
Step 22: Explain what the users said
  1. In the same "USER RESEARCH" section of your Word doc, click below the answers you just typed.
  2. Type a new sub-title: Summary of Feedback and make it a "Heading 2".
  3. Write one paragraph that explains what you learned in general.
  4. Example: In general, all 4 people were confused about the sign's main message. The main weakness, mentioned by 3 out of 4 people, was that the text was too small. The main strength was that it was colorful, but the symbol I used was not effective.
Step 23: Under a section called Data Analysis...
  1. In your Word document, create a new section. Type Data Analysis and make it a "Heading 1".
  2. This is where you use the methodology you picked in Step 18 (SWOT or ACCESSFM) and fill it in using the answers from Step 22.
  3. If you chose ACCESSFM: Write down the list (Aesthetics, Cost, etc.) and explain what needs to be fixed.
  4. If you chose SWOT: Write down the 4 categories (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats) and explain them.
Step 24: Create a short design brief
  1. A "Design Brief" is a short, simple paragraph that explains your entire plan.
  2. In your Word document, create a new section. Type Design Brief and make it a "Heading 1".
  3. Now, write one paragraph (between 50 and 100 words) that answers the 4 questions from the prompt (Problem, Who has it, Purpose, User, Context).
Step 25: Create a list of specs for your design
  1. "Specs" (specifications) are a list of rules your new design must follow to be successful.
  2. The prompt warns you NOT to use the exact examples it gives.
  3. In your Word document, create a new section. Type Design Specifications and make it a "Heading 1".
  4. Write a bullet-point list of 5-7 new rules for your design based on your data analysis.

PART 3: Making the New Design

Step 26: Using Canva, redo your sign
  1. Go to the website Canva.com. Log in or create a free account.
  2. Start a new, blank design.
  3. Now, look at your list of "Specs" from Step 25.
  4. Build your new sign in Canva, following every single rule you wrote.
  5. When you are finished, click the "Share" or "Download" button. Save the new sign to your computer as a PNG or JPG file.
Step 27: Ask your friends about comparing
  1. Find 1 or 2 friends.
  2. Show them your OLD sign (the "bad" original one).
  3. Now, show them your NEW sign (the one you just made on Canva).
  4. Ask them questions like: "Is this new sign better than the old one? Why?"
  5. Listen very carefully to see if their feedback matches your improvements.
Step 28: Write down the highlights
  1. Go back to your Word document for the last time.
  2. Create a final new section. Type Final Review and Feedback and make it a "Heading 1".
  3. Write one last paragraph about what your friends said in Step 27.
  4. Example: Our partners reviewed our new sign and they found that it was 'much clearer'. This contrasts with what the first group said about the original sign...
