GA 2: Project Deliverables & Roadmap

A Step-by-Step Guide for Industrial Revolution Machine Research

Step 0: Document Setup (Do this first!)

Before you type a single word, set up your "Environment" so you don't run out of pages later.

Font & Spacing
  • Font: Times New Roman
  • Size: 12pt for body / 14pt for headings
  • Line Spacing: 1.5 lines
  • Margins: Normal (1 inch / 2.54 cm all around)
Mandatory Elements
  • Header: Your Names (Top Right)
  • Footer: Page Number (Center)
  • Table of Contents: Use References > Table of Contents
1. Introduction: The Industrial Need
Length: 300 - 450 words Target: ~0.75 Page
Thinking Questions Why are we studying this specific machine? What was the manual labor like before this invention? What is the "Goal" of your project simulation?
Step-by-Step Paragraph 1: History & Context.
Paragraph 2: The specific problem (e.g. mine flooding).
Paragraph 3: Objectives of this paper.
Level Up: Quote a historical figure or a patent description from that era to show deep research.
2. Design Requirements & Specs
Length: 200 words + 1 Table Target: ~1 Page
Thinking Questions How do we know if the machine is "good"? Does it need to be made of iron to handle steam pressure? What are the exact dimensions (height, width)?
Step-by-Step List 5 "Requirements" (functional goals). Then, create a Table with 3 columns: Component, Material, and Technical Dimension.
Level Up: Use engineering units (kg, Pascals, Watts) to define your specs rather than just saying "strong" or "fast."
3. Design Brief (1800s POV)
Length: 250 - 350 words Target: ~0.5 Page
Thinking Questions If you were an engineer in the year 1830, how would you pitch this to a factory owner? Why is your design a good investment?
Step-by-Step Write this as a formal letter. Use words like "Sir," "Henceforth," and "Apparatus." Explain the *efficiency* gain over human labor.
Level Up: Mention "Reliability." Factory owners in the 1800s feared machines breaking down; tell them why yours won't.
4. Drafts & MotionGen Simulation
Visual Heavy Section Target: 1.5 - 2 Pages
Thinking Questions Does your draft look like a blueprint? When you ran the simulation in MotionGen, did the "Trace Path" show the movement you expected?
Step-by-Step 1. Insert a photo of your hand-sketch.
2. Insert a clean digital drawing.
3. Insert a screenshot of the MotionGen linkage moving.
Level Up: Use arrows to label the "Input" (where power enters) and the "Output" (where work happens) on your simulation.
5. Analysis & Annex "Math"
Length: 300 words + Formulas Target: ~1 Page
Thinking Questions What do the numbers tell us? Is the machine powerful or just fast? Where does the most stress occur in the linkage?
Step-by-Step Write your text analysis in the main body. Put your step-by-step math (formulas) in the "Anex" at the very end of this section.
Level Up: Mention "Mechanical Advantage." Calculate the ratio of the input arm vs the output arm.
Remember: The total document must not exceed 5 pages (excluding the cover). Update your Index last!
