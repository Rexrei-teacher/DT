This exercise shows you how to solve a problem by understanding why someone is frustrated, finding the real reason, and figuring out what they truly need. Use this structure as a template for your own assignments.

1. üò† Identify a User Annoyance (Physical Scenario)

Annoyance: **Not being able to open a locker easily or quietly, especially when you are in a rush.**

Scenario: You are rushing between classes, your hands are full, and you have to wrestle with a sticky lock or a heavy door just to grab a book, making a lot of noise and maybe making you late.

2. ‚ùì Create Diagnostic Questions

Goal: Understand the specific feelings and moments of frustration using **open-ended** questions.

Question Category Example Open-Ended Questions
**Feelings & Impact** "How do you feel about yourself when you have trouble opening your locker while others are watching?"
**Context & Triggers** "Describe a specific time when your locker caused you the most trouble. What was the *one* thing you needed most at that moment?"
**Pain Points** "What part of the locker process (the combination, the handle, the hinges) seems to be the most difficult to manage, and why?"
**Alternatives & Solutions** "If you could redesign the locker to solve your biggest problem, what would the new locker look like, and how would it work?"

3. üéØ Identify the Root Cause

Root Cause: **The physical equipment (lock and hinge) is suffering from poor design, age, or lack of regular maintenance, causing parts to become stiff and misaligned.**

Reasoning: Lockers are often made with cheap materials and rarely checked. The door hinges may be rusty, making them loud and sticky. The lock mechanism might have tiny manufacturing flaws, making the sweet spot for the combination hard to hit consistently. This forces the student to use excessive force and time.

4. üõ†Ô∏è Identify a General Solution

General Solution: **Implement a Proactive Maintenance and Quality Upgrade Plan.**

  • What to Do: Establish a schedule for regular maintenance where all hinges are oiled, and all locks are tested or replaced annually.
  • What to Have: A system that uses higher-quality, **lightweight lock mechanisms** (like push-button combination locks) and **dampening material** on the inside of the door frame to reduce loud banging noises when closed.

5. ‚ú® Write Down the User's Need (The Magic Formula)

The Magic Formula: [User] needs [to do/have something] in order to [goal/purpose].

The Rushing Student needs **to open and close their locker door smoothly and quickly using minimal effort** in order to **feel calm and efficient during passing periods, and move easily to the next class on time.**

Formula Component Content

**needs [to do/have something]** to open and close their locker door smoothly and quickly using minimal effort
