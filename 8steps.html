Project Roadmap: From Theory to Structure

Why we write before we build:

In the real world, an engineer doesn't just start building with expensive materials and hope for the best. They create Technical Documents first. This allows us to find mistakes on paper (where they are free) rather than in real life (where the bridge crashes). These documents are your "Insurance Policy" that your 70N goal is possible!

Style Rules: Times New Roman | Size 12 | 1.5 Spacing | Max 5 Pages (plus cover).
Phase 1: First Assessment (Planning & Preparation)
1. Cover Page Phase 1
What is this? The official identity of your project. It presents your work to the reader for the first time. 1. Does your title sound professional enough for an engineering firm? 2. Is the photo of your project clear and centered?
MUST INCLUDE: Title, Name, ID, Date, and Photo.
2. Index Phase 1
What is this? An organized list of every part of your report, helping the reader navigate your documentation. 1. Are all 13 sections listed in the exact order? 2. Do the page numbers in the index match the numbers in your footer?
MUST INCLUDE: Table of contents with page numbers.
3. Introduction Phase 1
What is this? A high-level explanation of the science behind the project and why you are building this bridge. 1. How do triangles help distribute weight differently than squares? 2. Why is spaghetti a challenging material for an engineer to use?
MUST INCLUDE: Explanation of truss physics and project goals.
4. Design Requirements Phase 1
What is this? A formal list of the constraints or "boundaries" that your design must stay within. 1. What are the specific limits you must obey so you don't get disqualified? 2. Why is the 70N load the most important requirement?
MUST INCLUDE: 70N Load, 0.25m Span, and 0.25kg Mass limit.
6. Design Brief Phase 1
What is this? Your logical strategy. Here, you explain your plan to solve the engineering problem. 1. What is your "Secret Strategy" for making the bridge stay under the 0.25kg weight limit? 2. How did the JHU simulator change your original idea?
MUST INCLUDE: Your logic for reinforcement and truss type choice.
7. Drafts Phase 1
What is this? The visual evolution of your bridge, from a rough concept to a precise technical drawing. 1. How does your final neat blueprint differ from your first messy sketch? 2. Are the measurements on your drawing clear enough for someone else to build it?
MUST INCLUDE: Hand sketch and final blueprint with measurements in meters.
8. Simulations Phase 1
What is this? Computer-aided testing to predict exactly which parts of the bridge will pull (tension) or push (compression). 1. Which beam in the simulation is under the highest "Compression" (Red)? 2. How much force is the simulator putting on the center node?
MUST INCLUDE: Screenshot from JHU Truss Simulator with forces visible.
Phase 2: Second Assessment (Testing & Reporting)
5. Design Specs Phase 2
What is this? The final "as-built" data. It records what actually happened during the build process. 1. Did you end up using more or less material than you originally planned? 2. What is the exact final weight of the bridge in kilograms?
MUST INCLUDE: Final material list and precise dimensions.
9. Analysis Phase 2
What is this? A critical comparison between your computer model and the real-world results. 1. Did the bridge break at a joint (glue) or in the middle of a beam (spaghetti)? 2. How close was the 70N real test to the JHU computer simulation?
MUST INCLUDE: Comparison of simulation predictions vs. real-life test.
10. Bibliography Phase 2
What is this? Academic honesty. It lists every source of information that helped you succeed. 1. Besides JHU, what other websites helped you understand bridge shapes? 2. Did you use any specific books or videos to learn how to glue the nodes?
MUST INCLUDE: Full list of all resources used.
11. Exhibit: Math Anex Phase 2
What is this? The mathematical proof. It shows the detailed calculations that justify your design choices. 1. What is the Efficiency number for your bridge (70N divided by its mass)? 2. Based on the force from the simulation, was the spaghetti stressed to its limit?
MUST INCLUDE: Calculated Efficiency and Stress using the formulas below.
12. Header & 13. Footer
What is this? The formal "uniform" for your document. It ensures every page is professional and tracked. 1. Is your name at the top of every single page? 2. Does the page count go from 1 to 5 correctly at the bottom?
MUST INCLUDE: Name/ID at top, Page Number at bottom.

Spaghetti Count Strategy (SI Units)

Spaghetti Strength (\(\sigma_{u}\)) is roughly \(15,000,000 \text{ Pa}\).

Step 1: The Force (\(F\))
Get the force in Newtons from your JHU simulation.
Step 2: Total Area Needed (\(A\))
$$A = \frac{F}{15,000,000}$$ Result is in \(m^2\).
Step 3: Area of one 1.7mm Strand (\(a\))
Radius \(r = 0.0017 \div 2 = 0.00085 \text{ m}\). $$a = \pi \times (0.00085)^2 \approx 0.00000227 \text{ m}^2$$
Step 4: The Final Count (\(n\))
Divide the total area by the strand area: $$n = \frac{A}{0.00000227}$$ This is how many sticks you glue together for that beam!
