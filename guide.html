IGCSE D&T Project Checklist

1) Open ChatGPT.
2) Log in to ChatGPT.
3) Create a new conversation.
4) Activate Study and Learn mode (Click on the + and then Study and Learn).
5) Introduce the prompt: "I need you to explain to me from the point of view of IGCSE 0445 Design & Technology what ______________ is and how it is used. (place the topic to study in the blank space)"
6) Read, understand, ask, and interact with ChatGPT to obtain relevant information on the topic.
7) Provide a definition and uses of the topic to ChatGPT with this prompt: "Ok, let me see if I understood: (here goes your DEFINITION AND USE of the topic), verify if it is right or not, if it is right, tell me that I can now put it in the Word doc, if it is wrong, only tell me what I should improve, do not give me a new definition but explain where I am going wrong"
8) Repeat in a new conversation for each topic.
9) ACCESSFM
10) SWOT analysis
11) Design Brief
12) Design Specs
13) Data Analysis
14) User Research
15) Create a Word DOC.
16) By using styles, organize the Word doc with index, titles, Title 1, Title 2, header and footer (and background for 9th grade).
17) Create a new section titled "Application".
18) Select one methodology (SWOT or ACCESSFM).
19) Explain why you chose that.
20) Create a survey (max 3 questions) to gather information to fill your chosen methodology (e.g., "what do you think this sign needs to change?" or "what do you understand by this sign?").
21) Ask MAXIMUM 4 people those questions and store them in the Word doc under a section called "USER RESEARCH".
22) Explain what the users said in general about your sign, making clear its strengths and weaknesses (same section).
23) Under a section called "Data Analysis", write which elements (according to your methodology) must be improved (e.g., Aesthetics, Cost, Safety for ACCESSFM or state the Opportunities and Weaknesses for SWOT).
24) Create a short design brief (50 - 100 words) following the steps:
  • A clear statement of the problem: What is wrong, missing, or could be improved?
  • Who has the problem (the user or client)?
  • Purpose of the design: State what you intend to design.
  • Intended user or target market: Who will use it?
  • Context of use: Where and how will it be used?
25) Create a list of specs for your design. (Example provided: Function, Materials, Visibility, Safety, Aesthetics, Maintenance). YOU CAN NOT USE THOSE.
26) Using Canva, redo your sign with all the corrections.
27) Ask your friends to compare both signs and check if there was any improvement and check if what you said about the first sign is the same as they have.
28) Write down the highlights of that interaction (e.g., "our partners reviewed our sign and they found...", "and that contrasts with what people said...", "so in the revised version we found that...").
